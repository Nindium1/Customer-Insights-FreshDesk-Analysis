
# Customer Support Analytics & Workflow Optimization
### Data Analytics & Customer Insights Internship — Excelerate (Jan 2026)

> **Role:** Global Team Lead | **Tools:** Google Sheets, Excel, FreshDesk, PowerBi | **Duration:** 4 Weeks

---

## Project Overview

This project involved end-to-end analysis of customer support operations using a FreshDesk ticket dataset. As Global Team Lead, I coordinated a 6-person international team through four weeks of structured analysis — from raw ticket categorization to executive-level recommendations and automation design.

The goal: identify recurring support issues, surface operational inefficiencies, and propose data-driven solutions to improve customer experience and agent productivity.

**Core finding:** The support team was performing well under pressure, but 33% of tickets were technical bugs — meaning the support team was effectively functioning as a secondary QA layer. By targeting root causes rather than symptoms, we projected a 20–40% reduction in ticket volume through automation and preventive monitoring.

---



## Dataset

- **Source:** FreshDesk customer support export
- **Scope:** 30 support tickets (January 4–10, 2026)
- **Fields:** Ticket ID, category, priority, status, created/resolved timestamps, agent assignment, sentiment, tags

## Project Structure

### Week 1 — Ticket Categorization & Support Flow Analysis
- Classified 25+ tickets across four categories: **Technical, Billing, Account Access, Feature Requests**
- Assigned urgency levels (High / Medium / Low) and standardized tags (e.g., `access_issue`, `payment_failure`)
- Identified the top 3 recurring issue types by frequency and root cause:
  - Technical Issues — 10 tickets (33%) — app crashes, checkout errors, upload failures
  - Account Access — 8 tickets (20%) — lockouts, password resets, 2FA failures
  - Billing & Payments — 6 tickets (15%) — refund delays, auto-renewal failures, tax errors
- Designed a complete simulated support flow for account access tickets, including SLA timelines, decision trees, escalation paths, and agent response templates

### Week 2 — Data Cleaning, Tagging & Sentiment Analysis
- Cleaned and standardized raw FreshDesk export: uniform date formats, anonymized customer data, consistent priority labels, resolved missing values
- Implemented a **single-tag categorization system** for streamlined routing (e.g., `access_issue`, `invoice_download`, `profile_picture`)
- Conducted **sentiment analysis** across all 30 tickets:

| Sentiment | Count |
|-----------|-------|
| Negative  | 24    |
| Neutral   | 6     |
| Positive  | 0     |

> Key finding: 100% of tickets were neutral or negative — customers only contact support when experiencing significant friction, not for positive engagement.

### Week 3 — Visual Dashboard & Trend Analysis
- Built a **multi-chart dashboard** covering:
  - Ticket volume trends (daily line chart)
  - Issue category distribution (bar chart)
  - Priority level breakdown (pie chart)
  - Agent workload distribution
- **5 Key Observations:**
  1. Technical issues account for 1 in 3 tickets — the support team is acting as a secondary QA layer
  2. 150% ticket volume surge from Monday to Tuesday, sustained through Friday
  3. 57% of tickets are high-priority — the team is in constant fire-fighting mode rather than proactive service
  4. Perfect agent workload balance (10 tickets each) — human performance is already optimized; gains must come from automation
  5. 60% of tickets are problem-related (Technical + Account + Security combined)
- **Ticket Volume Forecast:** ~30 tickets/week, with low Monday volume and elevated Tue–Fri activity (~4–5 tickets/day)

### Week 4 — Executive Report & Automation Design
- Authored a full **Executive Summary** synthesizing findings across all four weeks
- Proposed **5 data-backed workflow recommendations:**
  - Preventive QA: weekly regression testing on checkout and payments → 30–40% reduction in technical tickets
  - Dynamic staffing aligned to mid-week volume peaks → 25–30% faster first response times
  - Expanded self-service knowledge base for account lockouts and billing → 20–30% reduction in inbound volume
  - Automation of repetitive responses via macros
  - Continuous performance review using live dashboards
- Designed a **FreshDesk Smart Automation Rule** for account access tickets:
  - Keyword-based auto-detection and tagging (`locked`, `2FA`, `password`, `access denied`)
  - Smart priority assignment (HIGH vs MEDIUM based on issue type)
  - Round-robin routing to account support team
  - Instant auto-response with self-help guidance
  - SLA timers with alerts at 75% and 90% thresholds
  - Auto-escalation to senior agents for unresolved or repeat cases
  - **Expected impact:** First response time reduced from 30–60 min → under 5 min; solves 17% of total ticket volume immediately
